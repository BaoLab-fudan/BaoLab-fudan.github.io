Research
Statistical learning methods for biology and medicine

Our research is organized around two complementary directions: developing the statistical and machine learning machinery needed to handle complex multi-modal data, and applying these methods to answer concrete biological and medical questions.

Statistical Learning
Multi-modal Architecture Design
We develop neural network architectures that can disentangle and integrate multi-modal or multi-omics inputs, learning shared and modality-specific representations that preserve biological structure.
Multi-omics Integration
We design integration frameworks that scale beyond pairwise comparisons, enabling joint analysis across more than two omics modalities simultaneously.
Contrastive and Self-supervised Learning
We create contrastive learning techniques tailored for multimodal biological data, allowing models to learn from unlabeled or weakly labeled data — critical in settings where annotations are scarce.
Biological Questions
Cross-omics Interaction Quantification
We develop methods to quantify and interpret interactions across multiple omics layers, uncovering regulatory relationships that are invisible when each modality is analyzed in isolation.
Spatially Resolved Transcriptomics
We use generative deep learning to improve the throughput, resolution, and dimensionality of spatially resolved transcriptomics — including reconstruction of 3D spatial gene expression from 2D data.
High-content Drug Profiling
We integrate high-content phenotypic drug screens with multi-omics data to predict small-molecule function, enabling more efficient drug discovery by bridging chemical and biological spaces.
